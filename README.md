**India CO₂ Emissions Analysis and Economic Growth Comparison using R (1996–2020)**

##  Project Description

This project analyzes the trend of carbon dioxide (CO₂) emissions in India over the period 1996–2020 and examines its relationship with economic growth (GDP). Using R programming and data visualization techniques, the study explores emission patterns, growth rates, emission intensity, and comparisons with other major economies.

The objective is to understand whether India’s economic growth is sustainable or carbon-intensive and to derive meaningful insights for environmental and policy analysis.

---

##  Objectives

* Analyze India’s CO₂ emissions trend over time
* Compare CO₂ emissions with GDP growth
* Study emission intensity (efficiency of emissions)
* Evaluate per capita emissions
* Compare India with top emitting countries
* Identify patterns in emission growth rates

---

##  Tools & Technologies Used

* **R Programming Language**
* Libraries: ggplot2, dplyr, tidyr
* Data Visualization: Line plots, Faceted plots, Dual-axis graphs

---



##  Key Visualizations & Analysis

### 1. India CO₂ Emissions Trend

* CO₂ emissions increased from ~800 MT (1996) to ~2400+ MT (2019)
* Slight decline observed in 2020 (likely due to COVID-19)

 **Insight:** Economic growth has led to a steady increase in emissions

---

### 2. GDP vs CO₂ Emissions

* Strong positive relationship between GDP and CO₂ emissions
* Both variables increase consistently over time

 **Insight:** India’s growth is largely **carbon-intensive**

---

### 3. CO₂ Emission Intensity

* Continuous decline over the years

 **Insight:** India is becoming more **energy efficient** and reducing emissions per unit of output

---

### 4. Per Capita CO₂ Emissions vs GDP

* Per capita emissions are rising slowly
* GDP is growing faster than emissions per person

 **Insight:** Standard of living is improving while maintaining relatively lower per capita emissions

---

### 5. Annual CO₂ Growth Rate (Faceted Analysis)

* India shows moderate but consistent growth
* China shows high spikes
* USA shows stable or declining trends

 **Insight:** India is still in a developing growth phase

---

### 6. Top 10 Countries Comparison

* China is the largest emitter
* USA emissions are stabilizing or declining
* India shows continuous upward trend

 **Insight:** India is a major global emitter but still lower in per capita terms

---

## Key Findings

* India’s CO₂ emissions have significantly increased over time
* Strong correlation exists between GDP and emissions
* Emission intensity is decreasing, indicating improved efficiency
* India’s per capita emissions remain lower than developed countries
* Economic development is still dependent on carbon-heavy energy sources

---

## ESG & Policy Insights

* Transition towards renewable energy is improving efficiency
* Policies like the Paris Agreement may influence emission trends
* Need for sustainable development strategies
